Who Is Serena Williams?

Serena Jameka Williams, born on September 26, 1981, is a revered retired professional tennis player from the United States.

Widely regarded as one of the greatest tennis players in history, Serena has left an indelible mark on the sport.

She held the esteemed world No. 1 ranking in singles by the Women’s Tennis Association (WTA) for an incredible 319 weeks, including a record-breaking streak of 186 consecutive weeks.

Throughout her illustrious career, Serena secured the year-end No. 1 player position five times.

Her exceptional talent and determination propelled her to clinch 23 Grand Slam women’s singles titles, setting a remarkable record in the Open Era and securing her place as the second highest on the all-time list.

Notably, Serena stands alone as the only player to achieve a career Golden Slam in both singles and doubles.

Serena’s journey in tennis began under the guidance of her parents, Oracene Price and Richard Williams, who also coached her older sister Venus.

She turned professional in 1995, and her first major singles victory came at the 1999 US Open.

From the 2002 French Open to the 2003 Australian Open, Serena displayed unparalleled dominance by consecutively winning all four major singles titles, defeating Venus in the finals each time.

This remarkable achievement, known as the ‘Serena Slam,’ solidified her non-calendar year Grand Slam and career Grand Slam status.

Following this impressive run, Serena continued to accumulate two more singles majors, but faced setbacks due to injuries and a decline in form.

However, beginning in 2007, she made a gradual comeback, reclaiming the world No. 1 ranking.

Starting from the 2012 Wimbledon Championships, Serena embarked on another era of dominance, capturing Olympic gold (thus completing the Career Golden Slam in singles) and securing eight out of thirteen singles majors.

Her triumphs included four consecutive titles from 2014 to 2015, earning her a second “Serena Slam.”

At the 2017 Australian Open, Serena clinched her 23rd major singles title, surpassing Steffi Graf’s Open Era record.

Following her pregnancy, Serena took a break from professional tennis and made an impressive return, reaching four major finals.

In August 2022, she announced her forthcoming “evolution” away from professional tennis and played her expected final match at the 2022 US Open.

In addition to her extraordinary singles success, Serena, alongside her sister Venus, won 14 major women’s doubles titles, maintaining an unbeaten record in major doubles finals.

Their remarkable partnership made them the most successful duo in major finals across all disciplines of the sport.

Notably, they achieved a non-calendar year Grand Slam between the 2009 Wimbledon Championships and the 2010 French Open, solidifying their claim to the doubles world No. 1 ranking.

Serena’s excellence extended to the Olympic Games, where she secured four gold medals—three in women’s doubles, a joint record, and one in singles.

Together with Venus, Serena stands as the only women in the Open Era to achieve Olympic gold in both singles and doubles.

Additionally, Serena triumphed in two major mixed doubles titles, both in 1998, solidifying her status as the sole player, male or female, to complete three Career Golden Slams: one in women’s singles and two in same-sex doubles.

The arrival of the Williams sisters ushered in a new era of power and athleticism in women’s professional tennis.

Serena’s exceptional career totals 39 major titles, including 23 in singles, 14 in women’s doubles, and two in mixed doubles.

She occupies the joint-third position on the all-time list and ranks second in the Open Era for total major titles.

Serena boasts the distinction of being the most recent woman to hold all four major singles titles simultaneously, achieving this feat in 2002–2003 and 2014–2015.

Furthermore, she is the most recent woman to accomplish the Surface Slam, triumphing on hard, clay, and grass courts in the same calendar year, a feat she achieved in 2015.

Alongside Venus, she remains the most recent player to concurrently hold all four major women’s doubles titles, accomplished between 2009–2010.

In terms of earnings, Serena claimed the title of the highest-paid female athlete in 2016, amassing nearly $29 million.

She repeated this achievement in 2017, standing as the sole woman on Forbes’ list of the 100 highest-paid athletes, with a combination of $27 million in prize money and endorsements.

Serena’s remarkable achievements have garnered her numerous accolades.

She holds the record for the most Laureus Sportswoman of the Year awards, winning it four times (in 2003, 2010, 2016, and 2018).

Furthermore, she was named Sports Illustrated’s Sportsperson of the Year in December 2015, solidifying her status as one of the most celebrated athletes in the world.

Serena also stands as the highest-earning woman athlete of all time, a testament to her immense talent and enduring legacy.

Serena Williams Husband

Alexis Kerry Ohanian, an American entrepreneur and investor, rose to fame as the co-founder of Reddit and served as its executive chairman.

He has made significant contributions to ventures like Initialized Capital, Hipmunk, and Breadpig.

Ohanian’s accomplishments in the technology industry earned him accolades, including being named in Forbes’ “30 Under 30” list for 2011 and 2012.

Recognized as the “Mayor of the Internet” by Forbes in 2012, Ohanian’s commitment to innovation was highlighted in Wired’s 20th Anniversary issue in 2013.

He was also featured in Crain’s “40 Under 40” list for business in 2015 and acknowledged as one of Fast Company’s “Most Creative People in Business” in 2016.

On the personal front, Alexis Ohanian is happily married to professional tennis player Serena Williams.

Their paths crossed in May 2015 at the Rome Cavalieri hotel, where they were both staying.

While sitting next to Williams at a table, Ohanian was invited by her to attend the French Open.

Surprisingly, he flew to Paris to support her, and during their time together, they explored the city, deepened their connection, and eventually fell in love.

Prior to meeting Serena, Ohanian wasn’t particularly interested in tennis.

Although Serena is a Jehovah’s Witness and doesn’t typically celebrate birthdays, she made an exception for Alexis and commemorated his 33rd birthday with a FaceTime call.

Later, in December 2016, Ohanian proposed to Serena at the same hotel where they first met, creating a romantic ambiance with a table for two and a pool deck adorned with roses.

Following the birth of their daughter in September, the couple tied the knot two months later in a beautiful ceremony held in New Orleans.

Serena dazzled in a ball gown designed by Sarah Burton from Alexander McQueen as she gracefully walked down the aisle.

Serena Williams Child

Soon after becoming engaged, Serena and Alexis Ohanian received the joyful news that Serena was pregnant.

Merely a month after Alexis proposed, they welcomed their daughter into the world on September 1, 2017, and named her Alexis Olympia Ohanian.

Choosing this name was a heartfelt tribute to Serena’s father, Alexis, ensuring his legacy lives on.

Moreover, the initials of their daughter, AO, hold significant meaning.

They symbolize Serena’s extraordinary feat of winning the Australian Open in 2017 while carrying Olympia in her womb.

By naming their daughter Alexis Olympia, the couple commemorated the beginning of their journey as parents and honored Serena’s incredible sporting achievement.

As a testament to their boundless love for Olympia, Serena and Alexis created a shared Instagram account dedicated to capturing and sharing the precious moments of their daughter’s life.

This platform serves as a digital scrapbook, allowing them to document and treasure Olympia’s milestones, joys, and memories as she grows.

Through this account, the world glimpses into the loving family dynamic that Serena and Alexis have nurtured, celebrating the beautiful bond between parents and child.

Serena Williams Age

In 2023, Serena Williams is currently 41 years old.

Born on September 26, 1981, in Saginaw, Michigan, Serena is the youngest daughter of Oracene Price and Richard Williams.

Among Price’s five daughters, Serena has half-sisters named Yetunde, Lyndrea, and Isha Price, as well as an older sister named Venus.

Serena also has at least seven paternal half-siblings.

At the age of four, Serena and her family relocated to Compton, California, where her tennis journey began.

Serena’s father took on the role of homeschooling and coaching both Serena and Venus.

Another influential figure in Serena’s early development as a tennis player was Richard Williams, a Compton resident who shared the same name as her father.

Richard Williams later went on to establish The Venus and Serena Williams Tennis Tutorial Academy.

Serena’s upbringing revolved around her family’s unwavering commitment to her and Venus’ tennis careers.

Their parents provided guidance, support, and coaching, with their mother also taking on a coaching role.

The remarkable journey of the Williams sisters, from Compton to the pinnacle of tennis, stands as a testament to their exceptional talent, relentless hard work, and the unwavering support they received from their family.
